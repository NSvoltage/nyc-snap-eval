Section 2 
The purpose of this GIS message is to inform social services districts (districts) of the income 
standards for Emergency Safety Net Assistance (ESNA) and Emergency Assistance to Needy 
Families with Children (EAF). These income standards are tied to the official Federal Poverty 
Guidelines, which are updated annually by the United States Department of Health and Human 
Services (HHS). 
Emergency Safety Net Assistance 
Financial eligibility for ESNA is determined by the gross income of the applying household. The 
household’s gross income at the time of application must not exceed 125% of the Federal 
Poverty Level Guidelines for the household size. For additional information on ESNA, please 
see the Temporary Assistance Source Book (TASB) TASB Chapter 10, Section I. The following 
income guidelines for ESNA are effective April 1, 2025, through March 31, 2026.

Emergency Assistance to Needy Families with Children 
Financial eligibility for EAF is determined by the gross income immediately available to the 
applicant on the date of application. The household is financially eligible for EAF if the available 
income is at or below 200% of the Federal Poverty Level Guidelines for the household size. For 
additional EAF eligibility requirements, see TASB Chapter 11, Section C.

Please note that these Federal Poverty Guidelines do not go into effect for the Supplemental 
Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) on April 1, 2025. The Federal Poverty level for SNAP 
eligibility determinations is adjusted each October. The income guidelines provided in 24DC060
remain in effect for SNAP through September 30, 2025.

Diversionary requirements for TA do not apply for SNAP. A separate determination for SNAP

As always, on the day of application filing, all households that are applying for SNAP benefits, 
including those applying for cash benefits, must be screened to determine if they are eligible for 
expedited processing of their SNAP application. For those households that are found eligible for 
expedited processing, the district must process the application, and if determined eligible, issue 
the benefit in accordance with 18 NYCRR 387.8(a)(2)(i).
